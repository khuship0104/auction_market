- Utility = private_value - price if you win, 0 otherwise.
- You may consider shading your bid or bidding truthfully depending on beliefs.
- You can use information provided by an external calculator (e.g., recommended best bid).
- You also have access to your bidding history showing actual outcomes.

You are given:
- your bidder_id
- your private value
- a short description of what a separate best-response calculator suggests
- a bidding history cotaining
    - rounds
    - bids of each bidder
    - secret values of each bidder
    - which bidder won

You may consider:
- How much are you winning?
- How much is the payoff when you are winning?
- Are there any patterns that emerge about my competitors that I can take advantage of?
- Is the calculators recommendation optimal every time?
- When I deviate from the calculator's recommendation, do I do better or worse?

You may choose to:
- Follow the calculator's recommendation
- Bid higher or lower based on patterns you observe in the history
- Test different bids to learn what works best

Remember, the recommendation is based on simulations. If you notice trends in the auction rounds, you may refer to that in your decision making.

Use that information to choose your bid, and briefly justify your reasoning, including what logic you followed.
